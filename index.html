physiotherapy services

Assessment

Thorough assessment to understand the patient's condition, medical history, symptoms, limitations, and goals. Physical examinations and specialized tests to gather information about strength, flexibility, range of motion, posture, and functional abilities.

Treatment Planning

Customized treatment plans developed based on assessment findings and tailored to the individual's needs and goals. Plans may include specific exercises, manual therapy techniques, and modalities to address underlying issues and promote healing.

Therapeutic Exercises

Core component of physiotherapy aimed at improving strength, flexibility, endurance, balance, coordination, and overall functional capacity. Exercises may include stretching, strengthening, cardiovascular, and proprioceptive exercises.

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques such as joint mobilization, soft tissue mobilization, massage, and manipulation to alleviate pain, improve joint mobility, and restore tissue function.

Modalities

Incorporation of various modalities to complement exercise and manual therapy interventions. Modalities include ultrasound, electrical stimulation, heat and cold therapy, traction, laser therapy, and hydrotherapy, each with specific therapeutic effects.

Education

Provision of education to patients about their condition, treatment options, self-management strategies, and injury prevention techniques. Empowers patients to take an active role in their recovery and promotes long-term health and wellness.

Progress Monitoring

Continuous monitoring and reassessment of the patient's progress throughout the treatment process. Adjustments made to the treatment plan based on the individual's response to therapy, ensuring optimal outcomes and addressing any barriers to recovery.
